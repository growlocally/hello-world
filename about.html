About  ·  A short note

A designer who treats the web as infrastructure.

Twelve years of designing and shipping web. The last six leading teams. Most of what I make lives downstream of a CMS, a component library, or a content model — and that's on purpose.

01 — Bio

I'm David Cho — a UX Web Designer and Manager at SurveyMonkey. I spend most of my time on the systems that sit underneath a marketing site: the page model, the components, the motion vocabulary, the analytics that says whether any of it is working.

I started in agency work — the kind where a site launch was the deliverable and the CMS was someone else's problem. After moving in-house, it became clear the interesting problem wasn't the launch. It was the next twenty-four months: how does a small team produce hundreds of high-quality pages without producing a hundred design debts?

These days I lead a small team across web systems and growth. We treat marketing, product storytelling, and the design system as one continuous surface. I write briefs, build out tokens and components, partner with engineering on the CMS model, and run the analytics loop that closes feedback faster than a quarterly review.

Outside work I cook a lot, run slowly, and keep a long-running Notion of every navigation pattern I've ever found notable. Say hi — I'm easy to find.

“I'd rather ship a system that's boringly correct for a year than a beautiful one-off that needs a maintainer.” — Working philosophy
